About SHEMA

Bridging Kerala & Shepparton

കേരളവും ഷെപ്പാർട്ടണും തമ്മിൽ

Shepparton Malayalee Association (SHEMA) is a community organisation representing Malayalee families living in and around Shepparton. We preserve cultural identity, support community connection and celebrate Kerala's rich traditions through cultural events, festivals and social gatherings.

Kerala is known for its scenic backwaters, rich artistic heritage and diverse traditions — a culture deeply rooted in hospitality, community values and a strong emphasis on education and service.

Many of our families contribute significantly to the region, particularly in healthcare — nursing, aged care and allied health services — forming an important part of the local workforce.

The Heritage of Kerala

കേരളത്തിന്റെ പൈതൃകം

A land of enchanting backwaters, ancient performing arts, and timeless traditions that have captivated the world for centuries.

Performing Art

Kathakali

The classical dance-drama of Kerala, known for its elaborate costumes, vivid face makeup, and powerful storytelling rooted in Hindu mythology.

Ritual Art

Theyyam

An ancient ritual art form from North Malabar, where performers are believed to become possessed by divine spirits, adorned with stunning headgear and body paint.

Tradition

Vallam Kali

The iconic snake boat races of Kerala, where massive chundan vallams race through the backwaters in a breathtaking display of teamwork and tradition.

Landscape

Backwaters & Houseboats

Kerala's iconic network of serene lagoons, canals and lakes, best experienced on traditional kettuvallam houseboats gliding through palm-fringed waters.

Nature

Munnar & Western Ghats

Rolling emerald tea plantations, misty mountain peaks, and pristine forests that make Kerala's Western Ghats a UNESCO-worthy natural treasure.

Cuisine

Onam Sadhya

The grand vegetarian feast of Kerala, served on a banana leaf with up to 26 dishes — a culinary tradition that embodies the spirit of Onam festival.

Martial Art

Kalaripayattu

One of the oldest martial arts in the world, originating in Kerala. Practitioners train in traditional kalaris, mastering strikes, kicks and weaponry.

Festival

Temple Festivals

Grand celebrations featuring caparisoned elephants, traditional percussion ensembles, colourful parasols, and spectacular fireworks at Kerala's sacred temples.

Heritage

Chinese Fishing Nets

The iconic cantilevered fishing nets of Fort Kochi, introduced by Chinese traders centuries ago — now a symbol of Kerala's multicultural maritime history.

Wellness

Ayurveda

Kerala is the heartland of Ayurveda — the ancient Indian system of holistic medicine using herbal remedies, therapeutic oils and time-honoured healing practices.

Cultural Heritage

Traditions We Celebrate

ഞങ്ങൾ ആഘോഷിക്കുന്ന പാരമ്പര്യങ്ങൾ

From grand temple festivals to intimate family gatherings, Kerala's cultural tapestry is woven with vibrant art forms, rituals and celebrations.

Onam Festival

Kerala's harvest festival featuring Pookalam (floral carpets), boat races, and the grand Onam Sadhya feast

Pulikali

The spectacular tiger dance of Thrissur — performers paint their bodies as tigers and dance through the streets

Thiruvathirakali

Graceful group dance performed by women in white and gold sarees during the Thiruvathira festival

Thrissur Pooram

Kerala's most iconic temple festival with decorated elephants, Chenda Melam percussion, and fireworks

Chenda Melam

The thunderous percussion ensemble using the Chenda drum — a cornerstone of Kerala's temple festivals

Pookalam

Intricate floral carpet designs made with fresh flower petals during the ten-day Onam celebrations

Kerala Cuisine

Puttu, Appam, traditional Sadhya — a rich culinary heritage built on coconut, spices and rice

Classical Music

Carnatic music and Sopana Sangeetham — Kerala's devotional musical traditions at temples

Strengthening Multicultural Harmony

SHEMA contributes to the broader community of the Greater Shepparton City Council area, helping strengthen multicultural harmony, community wellbeing and local development. We are proud to have the support of Cr Shane Sali, Mayor of Greater Shepparton.

Community Organisation

SHEMA is an incorporated association dedicated to preserving the cultural identity of Malayalee families in Shepparton and supporting community connection through events and gatherings.

Healthcare Contribution

Malayalee families contribute significantly to the Shepparton region's healthcare sector, including nursing, aged care and allied health services — forming a vital part of the local workforce.

Cultural Exchange

Through cultural events, educational programs and social gatherings, SHEMA bridges the gap between Kerala's ancient traditions and Australia's multicultural society.

Must-Visit Destinations

Iconic Kerala Landmarks

കേരളത്തിലെ പ്രധാന കാഴ്ചകൾ

From ancient temples to misty hill stations, Kerala's landmarks tell the story of a land steeped in history, spirituality and natural beauty.

Padmanabhaswamy Temple

One of the richest and most sacred Hindu temples in the world, located in Thiruvananthapuram, dedicated to Lord Vishnu.

Varkala Cliff

Dramatic red laterite cliffs overlooking the Arabian Sea — one of Kerala's most spectacular coastal landscapes.

Munnar Tea Gardens

Rolling hills carpeted with emerald tea plantations in the Western Ghats — Kerala's most photographed hill station.

Fort Kochi

A historic port town blending Portuguese, Dutch and British colonial architecture with Chinese fishing nets and spice markets.

Heritage Crafts

Handicrafts & GI Products

Kerala's artisans have perfected unique crafts over centuries — many now protected with Geographical Indication status.

Aranmula Kannadi

Legendary metal mirror handcrafted using a secret alloy — a GI-tagged marvel

Kasaragod Sarees

Handwoven silk sarees with distinctive designs from the Kasaragod region

Malabar Pepper

The "King of Spices" — premium black pepper from Kerala's spice gardens

Alleppey Coir

Eco-friendly coconut fibre products crafted in the backwater region of Alleppey
